The Old Town

Tallinn is famous for it's UNESCO World Heritage-listed Old Town. Take your time and stroll along the narrow, cobbled streets, admire the centuries-old architecture, climb towers, and peek into mysterious courtyards and archways – some of which hide romantic cafés, cute shops, or secret gardens.
You can read more here.

Toompea Castle

Toompea Castle is the seat of the Estonian Parliament. The castle’s tallest tower of the Pikk Hermann, is a leading symbol of Estonian statehood; the blue-black-white Estonian flag is raised every day at sunrise from the tower to the tune of the national anthem.
Read more about it here.

Kadriorg Palace and Park

The centrepiece of Kadriorg is Estonia’s only Baroque Palace and park ensemble, built more than 300 years ago on the orders of Russian Tsar Peter the Great. In the majestic halls of Kadriorg Palace, you can get acquainted with the Estonian Art Museum’s collection of foreign art.
Read more about it here.

Seaplane Harbour, Estonian Maritime Museum

Built in a unique seaplane hangar, the Seaplane Harbour is without exaggeration the most impressive maritime museum in the region.
Read more about it here.

Noblessner seafront quarter

The former submarine factory is named after its founders Emanuel Nobel (nephew of Alfred Nobel, founder of the Nobel Prize) and Arthur Lessner. There you will find a modern urban space, galleries, design shops, a marina, and restaurants where you can enjoy the sunset.
Read more about it here.

Telliskivi Creative City

Telliskivi Creative City is one of the locals’ favourite places to hang out. The former factory site is now home to numerous design shops, restaurants, and cultural institutions. Next to Telliskivi, across the tramway, is Kalamaja. This is one of the oldest neighbourhoods in the city known for its colourful wooden houses.
Read more about it here.

Rotermann Quarter

Founded in the nineteenth century by industrialist Christian Abraham Rotermann, this industrial centre has grown into one of the city’s most prominent oases of modern architecture where old and new are skilfully blended. The quarter offers a wide range of shops, cafés, restaurants, and leisure facilities, from beauty salons to sports clubs and cinemas.
Read more about it here.

Maarjamäe History Centre and Song Festival Grounds

The permanent exhibition at the History Museum in the picturesque Maarjamäe Palace tells the history of the Republic of Estonia from its birth to the present day. The Film Museum, located in the same complex, offers a glimpse into the history of cinema. Just a short walk away is Tallinn Song Festival Grounds, the venue of our beloved national song festivals, which feature upwards of 30,000 performers, and many other international events.
Read more about it here.

Tallinn TV Tower

The 1980 Summer Olympics in Moscow included a sailing regatta in Tallinn, and the 314-metre TV tower – the tallest building in Estonia – was completed for the occasion. Today, the tower is home to an experience centre where you can enjoy stunning views, indulge in a cup of coffee or a light meal, visit an interactive exhibition, and even walk suspended on the edge of the tower.
Read more about it here.
